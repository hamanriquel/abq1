ABQ-ALC COMMUNITY

Student-Focused

We are a student-focused organization. We are dedicated to providing a safe environment where our students can hone their academic skills. We focus on self-achievement; students work with their instructors to identify the skills they want to strengthen and create goals to obtain those skills.

Community-Centered

We are a community-centered organization. We work with community partners to ensure our students have access to the resources they need.

Teamwork & Collaboration

As a team, we collaborate to ensure the best delivery of our program. Together, we work as a team to pilot new programs, support each other in our roles, and create new ways to help our students succeed.

Compassion & Empathy

We are empathetic to student needs. We approach students with empathy and compassion wherever they are in their educational journey, and where they want to be/go. We don’t choose paths for our students but allow them to explore careers, college, and trades.

Adaptable & Flexible

As a small, nonprofit organization, we are able to be adaptable and flexible to the needs of the community. We take into consideration the variables and possible obstacles that affect our students’ lives including individual learning styles, providing class schedules to accommodate family and work responsibilities, and access to wraparound resources.

Equity & Inclusiveness

We serve all students regardless of learning ability, age, gender identity, or socioeconomic status. We put forth the same effort to every student to help them meet their individual needs. Our students come from all walks of life and we value their respective cultures, diverse needs, and perspectives.

Our Model

Community-Based Learning

Albuquerque Adult Learning Center, Inc. (ABQ-ALC) is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization established in 2010. Formerly known as Albuquerque GED®, Inc., ABQ-ALC brings over a decade of experience supporting adult learners through high school equivalency preparation, post-secondary transition, and career readiness services.

As a community-based, grass-roots organization, we focus on serving adults in some of the most under-served communities throughout the Albuquerque Metro area.

Our Staff

Gloria Rael

Executive Director and Founder

A native New Mexican, Gloria earned a Master’s in Public Administration from the University of New Mexico and a Bachelor of Arts in Business from New Mexico Highlands University. Gloria applies her passion to program development and management with public, private and non-profit organizations. Gloria has managed Adult Education programs since 2006 and founded Albuquerque GED®, Inc. (now ABQ-ALC) in 2010. Under her leadership, Adult Education programs include Literacy, Family Literacy, Career Pathways, and Pre-Apprenticeship IET programming. Gloria has increased enrollment by over 30%. She strives to find innovative ways to expand and address the unmet need in Adult Education.
